History of CSU Fullerton

California State University, Fullerton (CSUF), founded in 1957, is part of the California State University system. Initially named Orange County State College, it began classes in 1959 with just over 450 students. In 1972, it adopted its current name, reflecting its status as a major university in Southern California.Located in Fullerton, CSUF has grown into a diverse institution with over 40,000 students and offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs. Known for its strong business, engineering, and teaching programs, the university has a significant impact on the region's workforce. Its mascot is Tuffy the Titan.
